Religion can be, and oftentimes is, inextricably intertwined with identity. In my pursuit to explore my Vietnamese identity, I looked into capturing how Buddhism (a major religion in Vietnam) affects one's perception. The painting depicts the complexity of such a relationship; the organic shapes overlapping the figure presents the interconnectedness of how religious beliefs shape your identity.

A big part of my Vietnamese identity is the household environment I grew up in. Capturing a seemingly mundane moment of my grandmother sewing allows me to explore how little details shape me. From her traditional Vietnamese home clothes to the allusion to her heritage, she represents my connection to my Vietnamese identity. The warmth of the subject matter contrasts with the cold background palette, symbolizing my internal conflict with feeling fully connected to my culture.

A portal into Vietnamese culture: Tết, the Vietnamese Lunar New Year. The image captures the spirit of the celebration; its sense of good fortune and the arrival of spring is showcased through my household's festive vase decoration. The subject itself becomes a portal, connecting the celebration of Tết in America to the original event in Vietnam.
